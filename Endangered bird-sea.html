Endangered Birds on Sea

1. Balearic Shearwater

Status: Critically Endangered
Estimated Population: 3,000 - 5,000
The Balearic Shearwater is a medium-sized seabird that breeds in the western Mediterranean and ranges across the Atlantic Ocean. With only 3,000-5,000 individuals remaining, the species is considered critically endangered due to threats from introduced mammalian predators, bycatch in fisheries, and habitat loss. Conservation efforts include research, monitoring, and habitat protection, as well as public education and awareness-raising campaigns to reduce threats to the species. The Balearic Shearwater is an important indicator species of the health of marine ecosystems, and continued conservation efforts are necessary to prevent its extinction.

2. New Zealand Storm Petrel

Status: Critically Endangered
Estimated Population: 100 - 200
The New Zealand Storm Petrel is a small seabird endemic to New Zealand and is one of the rarest seabirds in the world. The species is critically endangered due to habitat destruction, predation, and disturbance. The current population is estimated to be between 100 to 200 individuals. Conservation efforts include habitat restoration, predator control, and monitoring of breeding sites. The species' survival depends on continued conservation efforts, and public awareness of the importance of protecting this unique and remarkable species.

3. Amsterdam Albatross

Status: Critically Endangered
Estimated Population: 130 - 170
The Amsterdam Albatross is a large seabird that is endemic to the Amsterdam Island in the southern Indian Ocean. With only around 170 individuals remaining, the species is critically endangered due to introduced mammalian predators and longline fishing. Conservation efforts include predator control, habitat protection, and monitoring.
