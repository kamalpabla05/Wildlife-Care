Endangered Birds in Cold Regions

1. Sooty Albatross

Status: Near Threatened
Estimated Population: 42,000
The Sooty Albatross is a large seabird that breeds on islands in the southern oceans and ranges across the southern hemisphere. The species is considered near threatened, with a stable population trend, but is threatened by introduced mammalian predators, bycatch in fisheries, and climate change. Conservation efforts include research, monitoring, and habitat protection.

2. Northern Royal Albatross

Status: Endangered
Estimated Population: 15,000
The Northern Royal Albatross is a large seabird that breeds on islands in the southern oceans and ranges across the southern hemisphere. With only an estimated 15,000 individuals remaining, the species is considered endangered due to introduced mammalian predators, bycatch in fisheries, and habitat loss. Conservation efforts include predator control, habitat protection, and monitoring.

3. Grey-headed Albatross

Status: Endangered
Estimated Population: 2,000 - 3,000
The Grey-headed Albatross is a large seabird that breeds on sub-Antarctic islands and ranges across the southern oceans. With only 2,000-3,000 individuals remaining, the species is considered endangered due to introduced mammalian predators, bycatch in fisheries, and climate change. Conservation efforts include predator control, habitat protection, and monitoring, as well as research into the species' ecology and behavior.
